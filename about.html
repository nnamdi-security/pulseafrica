Who we are

Data is how citizens
hold power accountable.

PulseAfrica is an independent civic opinion research platform built to give the people of the United States of Africa a credible, transparent record of what they actually think — and to put that record in the hands of every citizen, not just those in power.

Mission, Vision, and Purpose

Mission

Measure the truth of public opinion

To provide every citizen of the United States of Africa with an accurate, independent, and transparent record of national public sentiment — free from political influence, editorial bias, or commercial interest.

Vision

A democracy that governs by public mandate

A nation where no leader can claim a public mandate they have not earned, where every policy decision is informed by real citizen data, and where the gap between government claims and public reality is zero.

Purpose

Close the gap between power and the people

To make public opinion visible, legible, and impossible to ignore — so that the 450 tribes across 40 states are never reduced to statistics invented by those they elected.

Our values

What we stand for

Radical transparency

Every methodology, every weighting factor, every margin of error is published openly. We have nothing to hide because we have nothing to sell.

Absolute independence

No government body, political party, or private interest can commission, influence, or suppress a poll on this platform. Ever.

Inclusive representation

All 40 states and 450 tribes deserve proportional representation in our data. We weight aggressively to correct for demographic under-representation.

Privacy by design

Individual responses are never linked to personal identity. Your opinion is yours — we only publish what citizens believe collectively, never individually.

Data over narrative

We publish numbers, not conclusions. We describe what citizens believe — we never tell them what to believe or which leader to prefer.

Continuous accountability

We correct our errors publicly and promptly. Every correction is logged, dated, and explained. Our record is permanent and fully auditable.

Our story

How PulseAfrica came to be

The United States of Africa was founded on the promise that its people — all 450 tribes across 40 states — would finally have a government that governed in their name. But promises require verification. Public opinion polling is how a democracy keeps that promise honest.

When no independent polling institution emerged in the years after independence, citizens were left with only government-commissioned surveys that told the story leaders wanted told. PulseAfrica was built to change that.

2022

The founding observation

Three years after the founding of the United States of Africa, no independent polling institution existed. Government-commissioned surveys told the story leaders wanted told. Citizens had no neutral record of what they actually believed.

2023

Research and methodology design

The founding team spent a year studying international polling standards from organisations including YouGov, Pew Research Center, and Gallup, adapting their methodologies to the unique demographic complexity of a 450-tribe, 40-state nation.

2024

Platform launch — beta

PulseAfrica launched in private beta with 500 registered users across 12 states. The first poll — presidential approval at 6 months into office — generated 1,840 responses and was published in full, methodology included.

2025

National expansion

Coverage extended to all 40 states. Registered users passed 20,000. The platform ran its first multi-sector national poll covering presidency, economy, security, education, and healthcare simultaneously, producing 44,210 responses.

2026

Today

31,890 registered voices. 187,342 total responses across 20 published polls. 14 polls currently live. And a public record that no government press release can erase.

Platform goals

What we are building toward

01

100,000 registered voices by 2027

Expanding our registered user base to ensure every state has a statistically significant sample size on every poll we publish.

02

Offline polling access

Partner with civil society organisations in rural areas to extend polling access beyond internet-connected citizens through assisted data entry programmes.

03

Multi-language support

Translate the platform and all poll questions into the 12 most widely spoken languages across the United States of Africa to eliminate language barriers to participation.

04

Open data API

Publish all historical poll data through a free public API so researchers, journalists, and civil society organisations can build on our public record.

05

Governor-level approval tracking

Launch dedicated approval tracking polls for all 40 state governors, updated monthly and published per state with full demographic breakdowns.

06

Real-time trend forecasting

Develop predictive models to show where approval ratings are heading — not just where they are — based on historical trend patterns and leading indicators.
